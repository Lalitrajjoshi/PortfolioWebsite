Books That Shaped My Strategy

Good to Great

Author: Jim Collins

Key Learning: The "Hedgehog Concept" (understanding what you are deeply passionate about, what you can be the best in the world at, and what drives your economic engine) is a powerful tool for defining long-term corporate strategy.

Thinking, Fast and Slow

Author: Daniel Kahneman

Key Learning: Understanding cognitive biases (System 1/System 2 thinking) is crucial for improving organizational decision-making and mitigating irrational behavior in market forecasts and negotiations.

The Innovator's Dilemma

Author: Clayton M. Christensen

Key Learning: Successfully managing disruptive innovation requires creating independent organizational units, as traditional units are optimized for sustaining technologies, not market-changing disruption.

Movies & Business Leadership Lessons

Apollo 13

Genre: Drama / Historical

Business Lesson: Crisis Management and Resource Scarcity. The team's ability to innovate under extreme pressure and constraints highlights the importance of domain expertise and calm, decentralized problem-solving.

Moneyball

Genre: Sports / Biography

Business Lesson: Data-Driven Strategy & Talent Acquisition. The movie illustrates how challenging industry norms (conventional scouting) with superior analytics (Sabermetrics) can create a massive competitive advantage with limited resources.
